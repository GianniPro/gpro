What is Starlink

Starlink is a satellite internet constellation operated by SpaceX, providing high-speed internet access through their satellite to 32 different countries. It aims at providing global internet access.

When did SpaceX start launching Starlink satellites

SpaceX launched their first 60 Starlink satellites on the 23rd of March 2019, aboard a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket. They didn't stop there but they continued to launch more Starlink satellites. They're about 2,300 Starlink satellites in our skies today.

How does Starlink work

Starlink works through a beam of internet data, through the vacuum of space. Then, ground stations on Earth broadcast the signals to satellites in their orbit, which will come back to the user on Earth. Starlink achieves high internet speed by launching low Earth orbit (LEO) satellites this makes the internet speed faster and covers a wide area.

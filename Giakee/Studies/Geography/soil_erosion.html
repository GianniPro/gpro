Soil erosion can be defined as the gradual wearing or washing away of the earth’s surface. Soil erosion is the denudation of the upper layer of soil. In another term can be defined as a slow process in which soil particles are detached and removed by the action of wind, water and other agents causing soil to degrade. The situation can be so worst to the point where the land can no longer be farmed and most be abandoned.

Types of Erosion

  1. Water Erosion: This is caused by water (from streams, rivers, rain and so on) continually flows against a surface. Water erosion occurs when rain or snow melt displaces the soil on ground. The more soil particles are moved or transported away. Types of soil erosion caused by water include:

    1. Rill Erosion: This is caused by the accumulation and running away of water which carves narrow channels on the soil through which water flows.


    2. Sheet Erosion: This is the removal of uniform depth of soil over an extensive area by rainfall. It occurs where the land has gentle slope with bare vegetable.


    3. Gully Erosion: This is the removal of soil over steep slopes by rainfall, resulting in the cutting of deep groves called gullies. When gullies become deeper and wider, it forms bad land.


  2. Wind Erosion: This is erosion that is caused by the constant flow of wind against a surface. Wind erosion damages land and natural vegetation by removing soil from one depositing it in another.
  3. Glacier: Glacier are masses of ice or icebergs which results from snow, with temperature changes glaciers melt and moves slowly along valleys and mountain causing the scooping off the soil surfaces leading to loss of soil.

Causes of Soil Erosion

  1. Bush burning.
  2. Over grazing: Excessive eating of grass by animals to an extent that the land is damaged.
  3. Deforestation: This is the cutting down trees without replacing them. This is usually worst when trees are being cut down in large quantity without replacing it.
  4. Cultivation along slope.
  5. Shifting cultivation.
  6. Excavation.

Effects of Soil Erosion

  1. It leads to loss of lives and properties.
  2. It damage road and railway lines.
  3. Debris from soil erosion can lead to water pollution.

How to Control Soil Erosion

  1. Afforestation: This is planting of trees to control erosion.
  2. Controlled grazing.
  3. Enlightening people about the dangers soil erosion.
  4. Cover Cropping: This is the planting of legumes like beans, soybeans, peas and so on to cover the soil to prevent erosion.
  5. Making laws to reduce soil erosion.

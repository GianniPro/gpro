The word solar is gotten from a Latin word called “solus” which means sun. The solar system is made up of the sun and eight other natural bodies called planets. The sun is at the centre of the solar system and eight other plants revolve around the sun in their orbit (elliptical orbit).
The eight planets revolving around the sun are Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. Pluto is no longer regarded as a planet because it is too small, it is now regarded as a dwarf planet.

The sun is important to us because it gives us light which can be conserved as heat energy. Without solar energy or heat insulators, all life may not be possible on the earth’s surface.

What is a galaxy

A galaxy is a collection of stars. The stars we see from the earth which appear to be single exist in a group called “Galaxies” or “Nebula”. Each galaxy can contain up to 100 million stars. The galaxy in which the earth is known as the “milky way”.

What is a Satellite

A satellite is a smaller heavenly body which revolves around a planet. A satellite is an object that moves around a planet in a given orbit. The earth has only one natural satellite known as the “moon”.

    Types of Satellite

  1. Artificial Satellites and
  2. Natural Satellites

Artificial Satellites: These are satellites designed and made by man. The first artificial satellite was designed by Soviet scientists. It is known as “Sputnik-1”. It has a mass of 85kg and was launched on 4th October 1957. Since then, thousands of satellites have been put into various orbits for several uses. Artificial Satellite is also known as a man-made satellite.

    Types of Artificial Satellites include:

  1. Communication Satellite
  2. Monitoring Satellite
  3. Weather Satellite
  4. Navigation Satellite
  5. Geographic Information System
  6. Astronomical Satellite
  7. Search and Rescue Satellite

Natural Satellites: These are satellites that are naturally put into place without any human effort. An example of a natural satellite is the Moon.

Characteristics of The Eight Planets


    Characteristics of Mercury

  1. It is the smallest, hottest and closest planet to the sun.
  2. It takes 88 earth days for Mercury to go around the sun.
  3. Gravitational pull is very strong because it is the closest to the sun.
  4. It has no satellite of its own.

    Characteristics of Venus

  1. It is the second closest planet to the sun.
  2. It is referred to as the earth’s twin or sister because it is similar to the earth in size, weight and density.
  3. It takes 225 earth days to get Venus to complete one revolution.
  4. It has no satellite of its own.

    Characteristics of Earth

  1. It is the only planet that supports life.
  2. It takes the earth 365¼ days to complete a revolution around the sun. Note: Since the ¼ from 365¼ days cannot be displayed on a calendar, it is given one day on the calendar during a leap year, which is every 4 years, making a leap year 366 days. This is gotten from ¼ times 24 hours which is 6 hours and to make 6 hours one day you divide 6 hours by 24 hours which is 4. That is why a leap year is every 4 years.
  3. It has one satellite, the moon which revolves eastwards around the earth once in 27 days.

    Characteristics of Mars

  1. It is known as the red planet.
  2. Astronaut thinks that there is a possibility of tiny plants living on Mars.
  3. It takes Mars 687 days to complete a revolution around the sun.
  4. It has two natural satellites.

    Characteristics of Jupiter

  1. It is the largest planet in the solar system.
  2. Its surface contains some hydrogen and methane gases.
  3. The atmosphere around Jupiter is characterized by thunderstorms many times stronger than that of earth.
  4. It has 13 natural satellites.
  5. It takes Jupiter 12 earth years to complete a revolution around the sun.

    Characteristics of Saturn

  1. It is the second largest planet after Jupiter.
  2. It takes 30 earth years to complete one revolution.
  3. It has 10 natural satellites.

    Characteristics of Uranus

  1. It is about 50 times the size of the earth.
  2. Its movement round the sun is in a clockwise direction that is from the east to the west.
  3. It has 5 satellites.
  4. It takes 84 earth years to complete one revolution.

    Characteristics of Neptune

  1. It is a cold planet.
  2. It has 2 natural satellites.
  3. It takes 165 earth years to complete one revolution.
  4. It is the furthest from the sun.

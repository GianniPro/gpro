Meaning of Frictional Force

Friction is a force of opposition that exists between any surface that is in contact. Friction opposes the motion of two or more surfaces that are in contact. Examples are

  1. Tyre of a car and the road.
  2. The sole of a shoe and the floor.
  3. Between the comb and the hair during combing.
  4. Rubbing your two palms together.

    Advantages of Frictional Force

  1. It prevents cars from skidding off the road.
  2. It makes it possible for the brake on vehicles, aeroplanes and so on to work.
  3. It helps us to stand and walk without falling.
  4. It makes writing possible.
  5. It enables an object to stay in its position.
  6. Shaping of knife made easy with friction.
  7. It helps us to hold things with our hands.

    Disadvantages of Frictional Force

  1. It causes wear and tear in the engines, shoe soles and car tyres.
  2. Due to frictional force, noise is created by machines.
  3. Friction produces heat that causes objects to wear out or damage the machine.
  4. It makes pushing and pulling objects more difficult.

    Ways of Reducing Frictional Force

  1. Lubrication of object.
  2. Making the surface smoother.
  3. By polishing the two surfaces that are in contact.
  4. By streamlineing of body in moving object.
  5. Design modifications: This means changing the design of the moving object.
  6. Using of ball bearing: This means in a machine where possible, sliding friction can be replaced by rolling friction by using a ball bearing. Examples of an object that are used this way to reduce friction are bicycles, DVD players, blenders and used in so many more equipment and tool.

    Ways of Increasing Frictional Force

  1. Applying more force to the object.
  2. Increasing the weight of the object.
  3. Creating a rough point of contact.

    Types of Friction

  1. Solid Friction and
  2. Liduid Friction

Solid Friction: This is a type of friction that exists between solids. There are two types of solid friction. They are
• Static Friction and
• Dynamic Friction

    Static Friction: Static friction is the frictional force that exists between two solid surfaces that is stationary or not in motion.
    Examples of Static Friction include
  • The friction that exists between a shoe and the ground while standing.
  • The friction that exists between a table and a television on top of it.
    Dynamic Friction: Dynamic friction is a type of friction that exists between two surfaces that are in motion over one another.
    Examples of Dynamic Friction include
  • The friction that exists between the tyres of a vehicle and the road while moving.

    Laws of Solid Friction

  1. The first law of solid friction states that friction opposes the motion between two surfaces that are in contact.
  2. The second law of solid friction states that friction depends on the nature of surfaces that are in contact.
  3. The third law of solid friction states that friction doesn’t depend of the area of the surface that are in contact to take place.
  4. The fourth law of solid friction states that friction is directly proportional to the force pressing the two surface(reaction). That is

    Force ∝ Reaction
    f∝R
    R i.e reaction = weight = mass × gravity. It’s S.I. unit is Newton (N).
    f i.e force it’s S.I. unit is Newton (N).
    µ = coefficient of friction. It has no unit.
    Solid Friction i.e µ = f⁄R

Calculations On Friction on a Horizontal ⁄ Flat Surface

  1. A force of 20N is applied parallel to the surface of a horizontal table is sufficient to make the block of mass 4kg. Calculate the coefficient of friction between the block and the table.(Take g = 10ms−1 )
    Solution
    Force ( f )= 20N,
    Mass = 4Kg, Gravity (g )= 10ms−1,
    Coefficient of static friction µ = ?
    µ = f⁄R
    Recall that reaction ( R ) = mass × gravity = 4 × 10 = 40N
    µ = 20⁄40 = 0.5
  2. A body accelerates 8ms−2 for 3 seconds. If the mass of the body is 20kg. Find the coefficient of friction between the body and the ground.(Take g = 10ms−2)
    Solution
    Acceleration( a ) = 8ms−2
    Time(t) = 3s
    Mass(m) = 20kg
    NOTE: If reaction is given there is no need to find it.
    Reaction(R) = mg = 20 × 10 = 200N
    Force(f) = mass × acceleration i.e ma
    f = 20 × 8
    f = 160N

    f∝R
    F = µR
    160 = µ × 200
    µ = 160⁄200 = 0.8
  3. The tyre of a vehicle grips to the ground if the car moves with a velocity of 3ms−1 to 6ms−1 in 3s. Calculate the coefficient of friction between the tyre and the ground if the car weighs 300N. ( Take g = 10ms−2 )

    Solution
    Gravity = 10ms-2,
    Weight = 300N,
    Initial velocity( u ) = 3ms−1,
    Final velocity( v ) = 6ms−1,
    Time = 3s
    Weight = mg

    To Find the mass (m)
    300 = m × 10
    m = 300⁄10 = 30kg

    For "a" acceleration
    Recall that formular for force = mass × acceleration i.e ma
    f = ma
    But 'a' ( acceleration ) isn't given but the initial & final velocity with time is given. So, the formular that will be used to calculate acceleration will be
    a = v-u⁄t
    Now you substitute a as v-u⁄t in the formular
    To Find the force ( f )
    f = m v−u⁄t
    f = 30 6−3⁄3
    f = 30 3⁄3
    f = 30( 1 )
    f = 30N

    ∴ coefficent of fricion is equal to
    f∝R
    f = µR
    30 = µ × 300
    µ = 30⁄300 = 0.1

Friction on an Inclined Plane

Sliding force = Wsinθ
Stationary force = Sliding force - f
Stationary force= Wsinθ − µR
Stationary force= Wsinθ − Wsinθ⁄Wcosθ × Wcosθ
Pulling force = Sliding force + f
Pulling force= Wsinθ + µR
Pulling force= Wsinθ + Wsinθ⁄Wcosθ × Wcosθ
